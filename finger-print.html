Fingerprints are considered a reliable form of biometrics, meaning they are used to identify and authenticate individuals. Key aspects of fingerprints: Uniqueness: No two people, not even identical twins, have the same fingerprints. Permanence: Fingerprint patterns are formed in utero and remain stable throughout life, unless there is severe damage to the deep skin layers. Friction Ridges: Fingerprints are made up of raised ridges and valleys, which are part of the friction ridge skin.
